# Datathon 2026 Submission Guidelines

This document defines what each team must submit through the official Google Form.

## Submission Overview

Each team submits **one complete package** that includes:

1. Track selection (choose exactly one of the 4 Datathon tracks)
2. Final notebook file (`.ipynb`)
3. Final slideshow for judging presentation (`.pptx` or `.pdf`)
4. Team and project metadata (title, members, contacts, summary)

Submissions are considered final once the Google Form is submitted.

## Track Selection (Required)

Teams must choose one track in the form:

1. Education
2. Sustainability & Critical Infrastructure
3. Health Care & Wellness
4. Finance & Economics

Your notebook and slideshow should clearly align with the selected track.

## Required Files

Upload both files in the Google Form:

1. Notebook: `.ipynb`
2. Slides: `.pptx` or `.pdf`

Recommended naming format:

- `teamname_track_notebook.ipynb`
- `teamname_track_slides.pptx` (or `.pdf`)

Example:

- `byteforce_finance_notebook.ipynb`
- `byteforce_finance_slides.pdf`

## Notebook Requirements

Your final notebook must include the following sections:

1. Problem statement and hypothesis
2. Dataset description
3. Data cleaning and preprocessing
4. Exploratory data analysis (charts + interpretation)
5. Statistical testing and/or model approach (if used)
6. Results and conclusions
7. Limitations and future work

### Citation Requirement (Mandatory)

The **last cell in the notebook** must be a **Markdown cell** titled `Dataset Citations (MLA 8)`.

In that final cell, include MLA 8 citations for **every dataset used** (including Kaggle, government, institutional, or API sources).

Minimum citation details per source:

1. Author/Organization
2. Dataset title
3. Platform/Publisher
4. Publication date (if available)
5. URL
6. Access date

If a dataset has no listed author or date, follow MLA 8 conventions for missing elements.

## Slideshow Requirements

Your slide deck should be presentation-ready for judges and include:

1. Team name, members, and selected track
2. Research question and motivation
3. Dataset(s) used and why they were chosen
4. Method summary (EDA/statistics/modeling)
5. Key findings supported by visuals
6. Limitations, assumptions, and ethical considerations
7. Final conclusion and recommended next steps

Keep slides concise, readable, and evidence-focused.

## Submission Checklist

Before submitting, confirm:

1. Exactly one track selected
2. Notebook opens and runs in order without major errors
3. Bottom notebook cell contains `Dataset Citations (MLA 8)`
4. All datasets used are cited in MLA 8 format
5. Slideshow matches notebook findings
6. Team information is complete and correct
7. Uploaded files use clear team-based file names

## Evaluation Alignment Tips

To maximize scoring quality:

1. Keep your research question specific and testable
2. Show evidence, not just claims
3. Explain chart insights in plain language
4. Distinguish correlation from causation
5. State limitations honestly
6. Make your work reproducible (clear steps, readable notebook)

## Academic Integrity and Use of AI Tools

1. Teams may use AI tools to assist analysis and writing.
2. Teams remain responsible for accuracy, originality, and verification.
3. Fabricated results, fake citations, or manipulated outputs are grounds for disqualification.
4. Any external assets (figures, templates, content) should be credited appropriately.

## Late or Incomplete Submissions

Submissions may be marked incomplete if required files or required citation components are missing.

If your team has a technical issue near the deadline, contact organizers immediately using the official event communication channel.
